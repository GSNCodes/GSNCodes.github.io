About Me

I am a machine learning and robotics engineer working at the intersection of 3D perception, multi-sensor fusion, and embodied AI. My work focuses on building unified representations for real-time scene understanding, depth and occupancy modeling, and uncertainty-aware fusion for autonomous systems.

I am broadly motivated by the question: How can we design embodied systems that learn, perceive, and act robustly in complex, unstructured environments? This interest has led me to research in simulation-to-real transfer, generative geometric priors, visuomotor learning, and scalable evaluation pipelines for long-horizon autonomy.

Beyond core perception research, I actively work on open-source robotics projects in teleoperation, navigation, and multi-modal reasoning, with a long-term goal of developing general-purpose embodied intelligence systems.
